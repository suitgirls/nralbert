Genevieve Grotjan

Nassoma/ July 23rd, 2024

Genevieve Grotjan is a cryptanalyst who made significant contributions during the 20th centuries. She is considered Americas first cryptanalyst. Genevieve Grotjan was able to crack thousands of codes and ciphers during World War 1, prohibition, and war War 2. Furthermore, she worked for the government more specifically the U.S Coast Guard. Her job consisted of interpreting and solving coded messages of mobsters and criminal gangs and she was able to monitor illicit smuggling rings.

Genevive Grotjan at first was a government mathematician. Afterwards, she was hired by the Army signal Intelligence service (SIS) in 1939. While working for SIS she was a cryptanalyst and was involved in decryption and remind of diplomatic messages. In 1940 she made a discovery that led to the breaking the “purple code” which was a cipher used by Japan during World War Two to ode diplomatic messages around the world. In 1943 she was assigned to the soviet problem and work on a project known as “Venona”. Within this project she developed a process for recognizing the re-use of key. The information that Genevive decoded was passed on to policy makers and senior government officials for decisions during war periods.
